Cosmic Egos

In the days of old, the deity charged with the control of deities; Placed a strict law, preventing gods from visiting the material plane. As such, archdevils, archfey, demon lords, and demigods moved about, free of worry to defend their territory.

As this environment came to harm against mortals, Ao, the overgod, chose to grant a degree of mortality to any higher being who walked this world, including even the mightiest of deities who chose to risk their divinity. The prospect of portfolios being up for grabs encouraged the most daring gods to send an avatar, if not travel here themselves, to stake a claim anywhere they can manage. This messy collection of the strongest egos in the multiverse caused what we all "Divine Convergence", which is the risk of an overgod forming. This threat spawning from the mutability of the divine.

Despite many travelling gods finding themselves in Olara, if at least by influence, an ever-evolving pantheon still claims the realm as their own. These gods still fall under the jurisdiction of Ao, but find themselves in a soft hierarchy contained to the gods choosing to stay in Olara. Loosing ranked by religious scholars, the gods fall into five distinct categories.



Ascendance

From most to least powerful, the divine are ranked as Greater Deity, Intermediate Deity, Lesser Deity, Quasi-Deity, or Demigods.

1.

The most powerful deities of the realm, Greater deities often come from other worlds and bring with them influence and power. Well above their weaker counterparts, greater deities are worshipped by massive religions or they have a portfolio which makes them a part of most mortal's lives.

Examples include Lathander, Shar, Faer, Bane, or Mystra.

2.

Power beyond power, Intermediate deities are usually in control of multiple minor domains or one major domain. Generally with followers numbering in the hundreds of thousands, Intermediate deities have more power than lesser, quasi, or demi gods.

Some popular Intermediate deities are Helm, Umberlee, or Celestian.

3.

The weakest of the gods, lesser deities are lesser known and lesser worshipped. These deities generally have up to one hundred thousand followers, but can have as low as a few thousand. Often contained within their own realms, the influence they have is usually kept within a well-defined group, demographic, or region. These gods include local patron deities, racial patrons, or those with strange portfolios.

Some examples of these lesser gods are Lolth, Maglubyet, or Malar.

4.

The lowest of those who can hold unique divine power, Quasi-Deities have few, if any, followers and rarely have dedicated site of worship. These deities are sometimes overlapped by demigods with the difference generally being the functional immortality held by Quasi-Deities. Powerful Archfey, Demon Lords, ArchDevils, and Ascendant True Dragons are considered Quasi-Deities, if not just equally powerful.

These beings include the Faerie Queen, Orcus, Zariel, or the aspects of Tiamat and Bahamut.

5.

The most powerful mortals to ever exist, demigods often are outlived by the legends they inspire. While unlikely, it is possible for demigods to steal the divine spark of higher powers if felled. Demigods come and go as generations move on. Well within the realm of someone who could be defeated by powerful adventurers at the top of their game, Demigods do not last long unless backed by higher powers or rock-solid assistance.

One can find Demigods among Legendary heroes, Greatwyrms, and Scions of the Giants.
