Seelie Courts

  • Summer Court
    • These fey are associated with warmth, light, and love.
    • Generally considered the "friendly" fey.
  • Spring Court
    • Powers relating to rebirth, change, and beauty ally to the spring court.
    • These fey are often joyful and welcoming.
  • Court of Thorns
    • Like the defenses of a rose, these fey are associated with constriction, toxicity, and deceit.
    • These fey tend to prefer subversion over confrontation, lending to their devisive reputation.
  • Wayfaring Court
    • These fey associate themselves with travel, paths, crossroads, and strangers.
    • Often found near busy hubs of travel, these creatures are observed meandering and directionless; sometimes willing to tempt others to act the same.

Neutral Courts

  • Court of Stars
    • Innumerable as the stars themselves, fey who find themselves curious can find a place within the Court of Stars.
    • Strong associations to adventure, the cosmos, and discovery; These fey tend to deal in knowledge.
  • Coral Court
    • To know the sea is to fear it. These fey are fearsome and powerful, although much more straightforward then their drier counterparts.
    • Dealing in not only the sea, but also warfare; These fey exhibit unstoppable might and undeniable fortitude.
  • Shadow Court
    • One might assume these fey are aligned to the darkness of the Unseelie, but even the Unseelie court knows the difference between a lack of light and the shadow itself.
    • These fey are the product of opposition itself. Associated with the void, as a concept for the lack of things, and with secrets; These fey are malicious natured, if nature even plays a role.

Unseelie Courts

  • Winter Court
    • These fey are archetypally cold, unforgiving, and dark.
    • Not outright evil by nature, these fey form socieites and covens more often to accomplish goals than to enjoy one another's company.
  • Court of Bones
    • The body, the earth, and time itself are some of the associations made with the court of Bones.
    • These fey can sometimes be appeased by something as simple as an impressive physique, others may decide they want the whole body for whatever purpose they may choose.
  • Autumn Court
    • The Autumn Court is very closely associated with death, decay, and rot. These fey find themselves preferring varying levels of solitude over the bustling intrigue of other courts.
    • These fey are hospitable to few and primarily associate with eachother for some semblence of security against those who may want to take from their domains.
