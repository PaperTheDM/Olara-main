Humble Beginnings

Before borders, before nations, before civilization itself; We dreamt of flight. For no other reason than from envy of the birds which flew just out of reach. Those foolish, free birds.

One of the first professions civilization spawned was to fight and return home to collect your pay. Since the beginning, bounty work has only continued to grow and change, from a bold group of those who would do just about anything for coin, to the organized and secretive groups that stalk the underworld to this very day.

No one quite knows how long the Crow Syndicate has been fully operational, but they have only continued to grow in power and influence; Spreading across the conquered world and gaining whispered renown.

Whispers of their successes and professionalism often reach those in power who would like less than legal actions to be taken in their favor, leading to many layers of secrecy between the buyer and the bounty, but with very high value work coming in.

Structure

Bounty work is messy and dangerous. For that reason, among others, the Syndicate organizes themselves discretely, putting together cloisters hidden within towns, cities, and kingdoms. These dens are called "Aviaries" to those within the ranks.

Each Aviary consists of atleast one "Magpie" or bounty distributor, and atleast two "Rooks" who protect their identity while providing the payout for work. A magpie may travel to neighboring aviaries if their own cannot afford to complete a bounty, creating a web of work across large regions. Contrastly, Rook are expected to stay near the aviary for both their own protection and to guard Syndicate secrets.

The primary force of each aviary are the agents, Crows, who take work. Membership to the Syndicate is shown by wearing a feather charm somewhere on their person, while expertise is shown by the color and material of the feather. The least experienced hold a white feather, the swashbuckling type may hold a blue feather, overland experts (trackers, hunters, etc) hold green feathers, assassins hold crimson feathers, thief archetypes hold brown feathers, and those who are considered capable of any type of work hold a Gold feather which give them some measure of authority over other Crows.
